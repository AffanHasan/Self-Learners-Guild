Example 11.1

A steel rod has a length of 10m at a temperature of 25°C. What will be the increase in length if the temperature is raised to 35°C ? Given ⍺ = 1.1 × 10−5K−1

Available Data

(Initial length of the steel rod) L = 10m
(Change in temperature) 𝚫T = 35°C − 25°C = 10°C = 10K
(co-efficient of linear expansion)⍺ = 1.1 ✕ 10−5K−1

The Solution

 Since we know that: ✫
𝚫L = ⍺ L 𝚫T 𝚫: Pronounced as 'delta' it denotes; The change in some quantity
𝚫L: Change in length
⍺: Co-efficient of linear expansion (Change in length per unit length per Kelvin rise in temperature. It's unit is K-1)
𝚫T: Change in Temperature
 Therefore Change in the length of the steel rod can be calculated as:
𝚫L = ⍺ L 𝚫T
𝚫L = (10m)(1.1 ✕ 10−5K−1 ✕ 10K)
(Change in length) 𝚫L = 1.1 ✕ 10−3m

Example 11.2

An air-storage tank whose volume is 112 liters contains 3kg of air at a pressure of 18 atmospheres. How much air would have to be forced into the tank to increase the pressure to 21 atmospheres assuming no change in temperature.

Available Data

(initial pressure) P1 = 18 atm
(Required pressure) P2 = 21 atm
(Initial volume) V1 = 112 liters
Final volume V2 = ?
(Initial mass of air in the air-storage tank) m1 = 3kg
(Mass of air required to increase the pressure of air-storage tank to 21 atm) m2 = ?
Note: There is no change in temperature

The Solution

 Since we know that:
P1V1 ÷ m1 = P2V2 ÷ m2
 From equation above:
(18 ✕ 112) ÷ 3 = (21 ✕ 112) ÷ m2
 OR we can say that:
m2 = 3.5 kg Hence the mass of air which must be forced into the tank is:
3.50 - 3.00 = 0.5kg

Example 11.3

Calculate the volume occupied by a gram-mole of a gas at 0°C and at a pressure of 1 atmosphere.

Available Data

(Given Pressure) P = 1 atm = 1.01 × 105 Nm−2 Temperature T = 0°C + 273 = 273 K ✫ Remember: While working with Gases it is good to convert the temperature from °C to Kelvin n = 1 (Number of moles) R = 8.314 J / mole.K ✫

The Solution

We know that:
PV = n R T From equation above:
1.01 × 105 V = 1 × 8.31 × 273 V = 1 × 8.314 × 273 / 1.01 × 105 V = 0.0224 m3 / mole V = 22.4 liters / mole as 1m3 = 1 × 103 liters

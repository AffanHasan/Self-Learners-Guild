Linear Thermal Expansion

Example 11.1

A steel rod has a length of 10m at a temperature of 25°C. What will be the increase in length if the temperature is raised to 35°C ? Given ⍺ = 1.1 × 10−5K−1

Available Data

(Initial length of the steel rod) L = 10m
(Change in temperature) 𝚫T = 35°C − 25°C = 10°C = 10K
(co-efficient of linear expansion)⍺ = 1.1 ✕ 10−5K−1

The Solution

 Since we know that: ✫
𝚫L = ⍺ L 𝚫T 𝚫: Pronounced as 'delta' it denotes; The change in some quantity
𝚫L: Change in length
⍺: Co-efficient of linear expansion (Change in length per unit length per Kelvin rise in temperature. It's unit is K-1 )
𝚫T: Change in Temperature
 Therefore Change in the length of the steel rod can be calculated as:
𝚫L = ⍺ L 𝚫T
𝚫L = (10m)(1.1 ✕ 10−5K−1 ✕ 10K)
(Change in length) 𝚫L = 1.1 ✕ 10−3m

Problem 11.2

A steel rod has a length of exactly 0.2cm at 30°C. What will be its length at 60°C?

Available Data

Initial length of the steel rod Linitial = 0.2cm
We have to find out length of the steel rod at 60°C Lfinal = ?
Initial temperature of the steeel rod Tinitial = 30°C
Final temperature of the steeel rod Tfinal = 60°C
Change in temperature of steel rod 𝚫T = 60°C − 30° = 30°C (co-efficient of linear expansion) 𝛂 for steel = 1.1 × 10−6 ✫ Value of 𝛂 for steel is taken from table 11.1 of the text book
In examination it will be given to you (there is no need to remember it)

The Solution

Linear thermal expansion in solids can be calculated by using following formula:
𝚫L = 𝛂 Linitial 𝚫T ✫ Linitial: initial length
𝚫: Pronounced as 'delta' it denotes; The change in some quantity
𝚫L: Change in length
⍺: Co-efficient of linear expansion (Change in length per unit length per Kelvin rise in temperature. It's unit is K-1)
𝚫T: Change in Temperature
 After putting values in the formula above, we get:
𝚫L = (1.1 × 10−6) × (0.2) × (30)
𝚫L = 0.0000066cm (this is the change in length of the steel rod) So, final length of the steel rod at 60°C will be:
Lfinal = Linitial + 𝚫L
Lfinal = 0.2cm + 0.0000066cm
Lfinal = 0.2000066cm Length of the steel rod at 60°C is: 0.2000066cm
